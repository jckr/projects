Fishing

Fisheries and aquaculture play a major part in local and national economies as well as in global food supply. The social and cultural role of fishing is critical in many societies, not to mention a mainstay of survival for millions worldwide. Making fisheries sustainable, while improving productivity and reducing environmental impacts of fish farming, is a major challenge.

How important is fishing in the worlds economy?

Fishers sell around US$95 billion worth of fish a year, and aquaculture sales are about US$106 billion. 45 million people are directly engaged full time or part time in capture fisheries or aquaculture.

The rise of aquaculture

Aquaculture is the fastest growing animal food-producing sector and outpaces population growth, with per capita supply from aquaculture increasing from 0.7 kg in 1970 to 7.8 kg in 2006, average annual growth of 6.9%. It is set to overtake capture fisheries as a source of food fish.

Trade in fishing goods

Fish and fishery products are highly traded, with more than 37% of total production entering international trade. Exports of fish and fishery products increased by 32% over 2000‑06. Exports of fish for human consumption have increased by 57% since 1996.
