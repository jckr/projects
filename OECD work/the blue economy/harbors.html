Transport

Maritime transport has been a crucial element of international trade for millennia and has become a driving force behind globalisation of the world economy. Container ships and bulk cargo vessels are the most efficient means of moving goods over long distances. Keeping shipping lanes open, making appropriate investments in port infrastructure and managing environmental impacts will be key to ensuring that maritime transport remains safe, reliable and inexpensive.

Leading players in shipbuilding

The global shipbuilding industry has experienced record demand over the past decade, largely driven by steady growth in international trade. Shipyards in China, Korea and Japan dominate the sector, followed by a number of much smaller producer nations in Asia and Europe.

Major harbours

The existence of efficient ports and port infrastructure is crucial to maritime transport. The advent of container vessels and dedicated terminals has pushed costs down considerably and altered trade flows. Countries worldwide will invest heavily over the coming years to further improve port infrastructure and increase their share of the trade flows.
